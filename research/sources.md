# AI-Powered SEO Content Production: Source Architecture
**Project Context:** Developing an AI-assisted SEO playbook tailored for B2B SaaS scaling, specifically mapping strategies for high-volume, database-driven sites like applicant tracking systems (e.g., 100hires). 

To ensure the resulting playbook is both highly scalable and highly resilient to algorithmic shifts, these 10 experts were selected to cover four distinct pillars of the AI-SEO supply chain: Architecture, Semantics, Risk Mitigation, and Conversion.

---

## Pillar 1: The Architecture Layer (Scalability & Programmatic SEO)
*Focus: Automating content creation for database-heavy sites (e.g., scaling "Job Description for [Role]" or "Interview Questions for [Industry]" pages without manual drafting).*

### 1. Preetam Nath
* **Profile:** Co-founder of DelightChat, creator of the "Programmatic SEO for 2026" framework. 
* **Primary Platform:** Website / Course material / LinkedIn
* **The "Why" for 100hires:** Nath's exact methodology—identifying repeatable search terms, mapping user intent, and using LLMs to generate hundreds of landing pages programmatically—is the precise growth lever an ATS requires to capture long-tail job search traffic.
* **The Conflict:** Nath advocates for massive scale and velocity. This must be balanced against Lily Ray's E-E-A-T warnings regarding "thin" programmatic content.

### 2. Jake Ward
* **Profile:** Co-founder of Byword, Contact, and Kleo. Runs high-profile AI SEO experiments ("SEO Heists") and builds programmatic tools.
* **Primary Platform:** LinkedIn / Technical case studies
* **The "Why" for 100hires:** Ward specializes in "by-the-numbers" SaaS growth. His frameworks detail exactly how to transition a B2B SaaS from zero to massive organic traffic using AI-assisted content pipelines specifically tailored for B2B lead generation.

### 3. Eli Schwartz
* **Profile:** Growth Advisor, Author of *Product-Led SEO*. Focuses on building organic growth into the product itself rather than isolated landing pages.
* **Primary Platform:** Podcast interviews / Newsletter
* **The "Why" for 100hires:** Schwartz argues that search-friendly features must be built organically into the product. For an ATS, the product (job postings, company hubs) *is* the SEO. His 2025/2026 push against "Answer Engine Optimization" in favor of true product utility is the blueprint for creating competitive moats against platforms like Workable.

## Pillar 3: The Human-in-the-Loop Layer (Risk Mitigation & E-E-A-T)
*Focus: Ensuring automated content complies with Google's quality thresholds and mitigates legal/reputational risk.*

### 7. Lily Ray
* **Profile:** VP of SEO Strategy at Amsive. Foremost authority on Google's Helpful Content Updates and E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).
* **Primary Platform:** LinkedIn / YouTube
* **The "Why" for 100hires:** Job requirements and legal compliance in hiring are YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) topics. Ray provides the required guardrails to verify that the AI-generated recruiting content meets 2026 E-E-A-T standards, preventing algorithmic penalties.
* **The Conflict:** Ray serves as the necessary counter-balance to programmatic optimizers like Ward, forcing a "quality over pure volume" check into the pipeline.

### 8. Aleyda Solis
* **Profile:** International SEO Consultant, Creator of *Crawling Mondays*.
* **Primary Platform:** YouTube / LinkedIn
* **The "Why" for 100hires:** Solis excels at creating technical AI workflows. She provides the frameworks for using AI agents not just to write, but to automate technical SEO audits and quality checks, ensuring the massive site structure remains clean.
