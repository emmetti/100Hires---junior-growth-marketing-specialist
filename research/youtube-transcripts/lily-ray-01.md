### [00:00]
SEO follows a predictable cycle: tactics work, get shared, get reverse-engineered by competitors, get targeted by algorithm updates, and get devalued. Google describes fighting spam as a cat-and-mouse game and a war they don't expect to ever fully solve. After 15 years in SEO, the pattern is consistent — every gold rush eventually gets patched.

### [12:30]
The 2018 Medic update introduced YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) as a formal category and elevated E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) as the dominant quality signal. Health, finance, and HR content — including job requirements and hiring compliance — operate under the highest scrutiny. Sites that survived did so by investing in verifiable author credentials, citations to academic or regulatory sources, and genuine firsthand expertise rather than optimized templates.

### [28:45]
The March 2024 core and spam update — the largest in Google's history — reduced unhelpful content in the index by 45%. Sites hit were overwhelmingly those scaling content rapidly across topics disconnected from their core product or service. Programmatic SEO and AI content pipelines that prioritized volume over utility were the primary targets. The pattern: organic pages scaling faster than organic traffic is a leading indicator of algorithm vulnerability.

### [41:00]
AI overviews are reducing click-through rates dramatically — from 1.41% to 0.64% for queries where AIOs are present, with further declines projected. However, LLM referral traffic converts at significantly higher rates than traditional organic. For B2B clients, LLM traffic is up 500% since early 2024 and converts at 7% on average. The strategic implication: optimize for citation in LLMs, not just rankings in SERPs.

### [55:20]
What search engines cannot take away: original data and research, firsthand experience, authentic community participation, recognized entity status in Google's knowledge graph, and cross-platform visibility. Winners in 2023-2025 share common traits — strong brand demand, direct providers of products/services rather than intermediaries, real user-generated content, and verifiable E-E-A-T signals. Don't play the cat-and-mouse game. Build things that can't be penalized.
