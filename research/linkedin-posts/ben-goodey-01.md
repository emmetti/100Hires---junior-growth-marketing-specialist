# How Typeform Built SEO into a $3M Annual Lifetime Revenue Channel

Typeform felt like they came out of nowhere. SurveyMonkey dominated the space, but Typeform's slick and intuitive UI put them on the map.

Jake Stainer, Typeform's Head of Growth throughout their scaleup, was new to SEO when he joined, but built out one of the most profitable SaaS SEO strategies documented in B2B.

**The core insight:** Embed the product into the content strategy. Don't create educational content that's disconnected from what you sell.

"I guess the trick is how can you relate your SEO to your product as closely as possible and not start creating like just educational content." — Jake Stainer, Co-Founder of Skale, Ex-Typeform Head of Growth

**What Typeform actually did:**
- Selected and prioritized keywords to drive revenue, not just traffic
- Built out a template library ranking for thousands of bottom-of-funnel keywords
- Used a unique backlink building technique that scaled them to 2.3M backlinks and 93 domain rating
- Built an SEO growth model that tied every content decision back to revenue impact

**The lesson for challenger SaaS brands:** Legacy platforms like SurveyMonkey dominated on domain authority and brand. Typeform didn't beat them by writing better blog posts. They beat them by connecting their content directly to their product's core utility — making the product itself the SEO engine.

For an ATS platform, the same principle applies. The job description pages, salary benchmarks, and hiring workflows are not content marketing. They are the product. Building SEO into that layer creates a compounding organic moat that a competitor writing generic "how to hire" guides cannot replicate.

The brands that win are the ones where the product and the search experience are the same thing.
